The DSM-5 is the diagnostic mannual that psychologist use. It serves as a way to identify behavior, or issues in thinking. Currently, it is the most widely accepted tool in assessing mental health that we have in the United States.
The diagnostic criteria for PTSD goes as follows:

  • Exposure to actual or threatened death, which includes but is not limited to:
    1. Experiencing tramatic events ourselves
    2. Being in person to witness events that impact others
    3. Having someone close to you experience a tramatic event
    4. Exposure to details of tramatic events
  • Pressence of intrusive symptoms such as:
    1. Intrusive thoughts or memories that occur more than one
      (In small children this can also manifest as tramatic themes)
    2. Nightmares, or dreams that cause distress
    3. Flashbacks where the person thinks or feels the way that they did when the trama occured.
    4. Distress at cues or things that are a reminder of the event
    5. Avoidance of stimuli or persons associated with the event
  • Changes in behavior associated with the trauma. They can look like:
    1. Anger or emotional outbursts with no apparent cause
    2. Hypervigalance
    3. Being startled easily
    4. Problems with concentration
    5. Issues with sleep such as falling asleep or staying asleep
  • The time period in which the disturbances occur is longer than 1 month
  • Disturbances cause issues in peronal relationships
  • There is not another condition that can attribute to the disturbances such as substance abuse or a medical condition
